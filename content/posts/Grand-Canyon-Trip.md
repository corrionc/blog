## Family Itinerary – Early Spring (South Rim)

5-Day Itinerary for Multigenerational Family (Grandparent + Adults + Kids under 10)

### Day 1 – Arrival & Easy Introduction to the Canyon

1. Arrive via the South Entrance and check into Maswik, Yavapai, or Bright Angel Lodge.
2. Walk the Rim Trail from Mather Point to Yavapai Point (paved and easy).
3. Visit Yavapai Geology Museum for panoramic indoor canyon views.
4. Dinner at El Tovar Dining Room; sunset viewing at Mather Point.

### Day 2 – Scenic Train & Village Highlights

1. Optional: Ride the Grand Canyon Railway from Williams.
2. Explore the Grand Canyon Village Historic District – Kolb Studio, Lookout Studio, and Hopi House.
3. Lunch at the Arizona Room; short walk on Bright Angel Trail for a taste of the inner
   canyon.
4. Evening Ranger talk or stargazing at Mather Point.

### Day 3 – Hermit Road & Rim Trail Adventure

1. Use the free Hermit Road Shuttle to visit Maricopa, Powell, Hopi, and Mohave Points.
2. Walk short paved sections of the Rim Trail between stops for variety.
3. Picnic at Hermits Rest, then return via shuttle stopping for photos.
4. Sunset at Hopi Point – one of the best views on the South Rim.

### Day 4 – Family Adventure Day

1. Morning mule ride along the Rim (2-hour gentle ride, ages 9+).
2. Alternative: Trail of Time walk near Visitor Center – flat, interactive, and educational.
3. Visit Tusayan Ruins & Museum to explore ancient Puebloan dwellings.
4. Drive to Desert View Watchtower for sweeping vistas and art displays.
5. Dinner stop at Cameron Trading Post on return route.

### Day 5 – Sunrise, Souvenirs & Relaxed Departure

1. Watch sunrise at Yaki Point or Mather Point – easily accessed via shuttle.
2. Breakfast at your lodge; complete Junior Ranger booklets and collect badges.
3. Browse Visitor Center & bookstore for souvenirs.
4. Optional Rim Trail stroll east of the Visitor Center before departure.

### Travel Tips

1. Weather: Expect 30s–60s°F; dress in layers.
2. Use free park shuttles to minimize driving and walking.
3. All major overlooks are paved and accessible.
4. Avoid steep descents into the canyon; stick to rim trails for easy terrain.
5. Bring refillable water bottles, binoculars, and sun protection.
